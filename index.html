About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Scientist in the Lightwave Research Laboratory at the Columbia University in the City of New York, supervised by Prof. Keren Bergman. I obtained my Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of California, Santa Barbara, in 2021, co-advised by Prof. Kwang-Ting Cheng and Prof. John E. Bowers. I earned my B.Eng. degree in Electronic Engineering from Tsinghua University, Beijing, China, in 2015.

My research focuses on the design enablement of ultra high-bandwidth, energy-efficient, and adaptable system connectivity in the era dominated by ubiquitous data. Addressing the prevalent communication bottleneck in contemporary distributed computing infrastructures, intensified by the proliferation of data-centric AI/machine learning workloads, I am specifically dedicated to designing—as well as democratizing the design process for—scalable link and system architectures that aim to fully unleash the potential of integrated silicon photonics, fostering a seamless synergy between communication and computation. With new opportunities arising from the unprecedentedly tight electronic/photonic integration, I envision a paradigm shift in computing system architectures that promises unparalleled interconnection bandwidth density, versatility, and functionality.
